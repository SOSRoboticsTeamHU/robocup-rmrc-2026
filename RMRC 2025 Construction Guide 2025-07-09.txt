Introduction
This document describes the purchase and construction requirements for the 2025 RoboCupRescue Rapidly Manufactured Robot Challenge (RMRC) Arena. 
Common specifications
* All timber is assumed to be 38x75 mm (±3 mm) in cross-section unless otherwise stated. 
* All OSB panels are assumed to be 15 mm (±3 mm) thick. 
* All polycarbonate sheets are assumed to be 5 mm (±1 mm) thick. 
* All dimensions should be within 3 mm of specified unless otherwise noted. 
* All angles should be within 2º of specified unless otherwise noted. 
* If the standard sizes of raw materials in the region where the arena is to be built do not match these measurements (especially timber), the builder may elect to substitute the closest standard-sized raw materials. This will require some cuts to be altered so that critical dimensions (indicated in the instructions for each apparatus) are maintained. 
* Screws and fastener quantities are not included.

Critical dimensions: 
Alternative construction materials and techniques may be used as long as the design is adjusted to maintain the following dimensions: 
* The inner dimensions of each half-pallet are between 300 x 1,200 mm and 305 x 1,205 mm.

2. 2x 600x600 mm panels, holes drilled as shown. 
  
x2

x4
4. 12x 550 mm long, 50 mm diameter PVC pipes. 
5. 1x 600 mm long, 85x85 mm (±10 mm) beam.

1. Assemble the 2 Hurdle Pallets. Note orientation of beams relative to holes. 
  
x2

x4

1. Assemble 60 cm Z-Pallet (see common components section).

4. Secure the 600 mm, 85x85 mm beam to the middle of the 60 cm Z-Pallet, either by screwing from underneath or by screwing at an angle (“toenailing” or “pocket holing”) from above. Brackets may also be used as long as they do not affect the fit of the notched pallets.

7. Day 1 - Low (easy) configuration: 
   1. As built, the lane is in Low (easy) configuration. 
8. Day 2 and/or Day 3 - High (hard) configuration: 
   1. Place remaining two Notched Hurdle Pallets, with pipes affixed, as shown. Do not screw down, they should be removable. 
  



NOTE: Additional all-parallel layout is being developed. This will not require additional parts. (The extra holes in the notched pallet enable this alternative layout.)

Alternative materials and methods of construction are acceptable as long as the following hold. 
* Each hurdle increment should be between 45 mm and 55 mm in height. 
* The apparatus fits in, and fills, the 60 mm Z-pallet as shown. 
* The pipes are free to rotate (with some friction). 
* Each panel can support 10 kg in their centers without deflecting by more than 10 mm.

Mapping fiducials (6 required)
  
  

Parts to cut: 
1. 1/2x PVC pipe (or concrete form, thick cardboard tube, trash can, barrel, or other similar cylindrical object) with a diameter of 250-350 mm, a length of 500 mm, and a diameter variation of less than 25 mm along its length, cut in half lengthwise as shown. (One pipe makes 2 Mapping Fiducials.)
   1. The material chosen should be strong enough to hold a 2 kg force as shown without substantially deflecting. If the material deflects slightly, double the quantity of beams (item 2, below) and secure them top and bottom. 
  

2. 1x Beam, length equal to internal diameter of the pipe (or other cylindrical object used). Ends should be cut at 82.5º as shown to facilitate a good fit. 
  

Assemble on-site: 
1. Screw beam into top of half-pipe (or other cylindrical object used). 
   1. If the material deflects slightly, also screw a second beam in the same manner at the bottom. 
  

2. Once the Labyrinth is set up, screw the fiducials in pairs, directly opposite each other on a wall, flush with the top. The locations shown below are an example, other locations may be chosen randomly as long as the robot is able, at different points in the mission, to see one, and then the other, of each pair. 
  

Critical dimensions: 
All dimensions should be within the specified tolerances.

Sensor crates and door (3 required)
  
  
  

Parts to prepare, print, cut, and purchase: 
1. 1x Crate (or box) of any color, with internal dimensions between 300 x 300 x 300 mm and 400 x 400 x 400 mm. Each face should have openings that total at least 30% of its surface area to let light in. (Note: For simplicity, the openings have been omitted in many of the other diagrams in this document and the rules document.)
   1. This may also be made from scraps of timber as shown in the example image. 
2. 10x Printed sensor test sheets that can be attached to the back surface of the crate and swapped out for randomization, available for download from here. 
3. Chemical hand warmers (sachets that heat up when you unseal and shake them) of between 20 x 80 mm and 50 x 100 mm. Enough to keep one at least 10ºC warmer than ambient for the entire competition and practice time. As a general rule, 4 per crate per day is a good starting point but this will depend on the exact specifications of the available chemical hand warmers. These may be substituted for other small heat pads of the same size, such as USB powered hand warmers. 
4. 3x round neodymium fridge magnets, 23 mm to 26 mm in diameter, 2 mm to 3 mm in thickness, and with metallic silver surfaces. 
5. Black and clear adhesive tape, at least 50 mm wide. 
6. Cardboard or similar as necessary, 50 x 150 mm (see below). 
7. 1x panel to fit the opening of the crate, drilled with 10 mm diameter holes that allow it to be hinged to the crate with zip ties. The dimensions below are for a 300 x 300 mm crate, adjust depending on the size of the crate. 
  

8. 2x Heavy duty zip ties, at least 200 mm in length as hinges. 
9. 1x Furniture/draw/cabinet handle with dimensions within the range as shown. Corners and edges may be rounded as long as they fall within the specified ranges. 
  

For example: 
  
  
  

Assemble on-site: 
1. Attach the sensor test sheet by its top edge to the back of the crate, so that the bottom is free to hinge upwards. 
2. Attach the chemical hand warmer to the back of the crate, behind one of the three marked section at the bottom of the sensor test sheet. The sheet should then hang over the hand warmer, just touching it. Ensure that the hand warmer cannot be seen from the front of the crate. The chemical hand warmer should be attached to the crate, rather than the sheet, to avoid distorting the sheet and giving away its position. In this example, the hand warmer (grey rectangle) is placed behind section “C”, on the far right looking from the front of the crate (far left looking from the back of the crate). 
  
     
3. Attach a piece of black adhesive tape, at least 150 mm long, along the inside bottom edge of the crate. It may be necessary to place a piece of cardboard underneath the tape depending on the pattern of openings in the crate. 
4. Using the clear adhesive tape, attach the three magnets in a line with centers 50 mm apart, each in a random orientation (North or South up), as shown in the diagram below. This should be secure to the crate well enough so that the magnets will not be accidentally pulled up by metal parts of the robot. 
  

Alternative methods of securing the magnets, such as with a 3D printed or lasercut faceplate, or gluing them to a piece of black plastic, are acceptable as long as the following criteria are satisfied. 
   1. The overall black area is at least 150 x 50 mm in size. 
   2. At least a 22 mm diameter circular area of each magnet is visible. This area must be either the natural silver color of the magnet, or white. 
   3. The magnets are placed in a line with centers 50 mm apart. 
   4. The difference in height between the top of each magnet and the top of the surrounding black surface is no more than 5 mm (above or below). 
   5. The difference in height between each magnet is no more than 1 mm. 
   6. There are no metallic fasteners (screws, bolts, etc.) within the 150 x 50 mm area or within 10 mm below this area as measured from the top surface of the magnets. 
  

5. Attach the handle to the panel as shown. Dimensions relative to the bottom left corner of the panel should hold regardless of the exact size of the panel. 
  
  

6. Use zipties to loosely attach the panel to the crate as shown. Depending on the construction of the crate, it may be necessary to drill additional holes to facilitate securing the zipties. Panel should open and close freely (apart from the lower edge dragging against the floor), without moving the crate.

Linear rail dexterity apparatus and manipulation objects (2 required)
  
  

  
                  
Parts to prepare and cut: 
1. 1x 600 mm beam. 
2. 1x 200 mm beam with ends cut as shown. 
  

3. 3x white and 2x black cups, PVC pipes with endcaps (painted if necessary), or 3D printed cups. Internal diameter should be 50-60 mm, internal length should be 50 mm, variation in internal diameter should be no more than 10 mm, thickness of base should be 2-8 mm. 
4. 10x soft foam cylinders or rectangular prisms, 20-30 mm in diameter (or 20-30 mm in square cross-section), 100-120 mm in length. These may be small foam paint rollers, pieces of insulation, packing, or upholstery foam, etc..
